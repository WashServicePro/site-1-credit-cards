Who is this card best for?

  • Frequent travelers and diners who want a single card that earns well across both categories — 3x on restaurants and 2x on all travel adds up fast for people who eat out often and take a few trips a year.
  • Chase ecosystem builders looking for a flexible entry point into Ultimate Rewards — points transfer to over a dozen airline and hotel partners like Hyatt, United, and British Airways, where the real outsized value lives.
  • Cardholders who want genuine travel protections without paying for a premium card — trip cancellation up to $10,000 per person and rental car coverage are benefits that regularly save cardholders hundreds of dollars.

Who should consider a different card?

  • Big spenders who travel frequently and want luxury perks like airport lounge access or an annual travel credit — the Chase Sapphire Reserve at $550/year is the more powerful option once you're spending over roughly $30,000 annually.
  • Anyone who carries a balance month-to-month — the 20.99%–27.99% variable APR means interest charges will quickly erase the value of any points earned, so this card works best when paid in full each month.

Our review

The Chase Sapphire Preferred has held its position as the most recommended travel credit card in the mid-tier category for good reason: it delivers a genuinely useful combination of rewards, protections, and redemption flexibility at a price point that's easy to justify. The 60,000-point welcome bonus — worth $750 through the Chase Travel portal and potentially much more when transferred to airline or hotel partners — alone covers the annual fee for years. Pair that with 3x on dining and streaming, and you have a card that earns meaningfully on spending most people do every day.

Where the Sapphire Preferred truly pulls ahead of competitors is in the quality of its redemption ecosystem. Chase Ultimate Rewards points transfer at a 1:1 ratio to partners including World of Hyatt, United MileagePlus, Southwest Rapid Rewards, and British Avios — and a skilled redeemer can routinely extract two to four cents per point on premium flights and hotel nights. That flexibility means the points you earn on your morning coffee or last-minute DoorDash order are worth far more than the 1.25¢ floor value. The 10% anniversary points bonus is a quiet but meaningful perk that effectively reduces the cost of holding the card each year.

The card's travel protections deserve more attention than they typically get. Trip cancellation and interruption coverage up to $10,000 per person is a real safety net for anyone who books nonrefundable flights or tours — a single claim can pay for years of annual fees. The auto rental collision damage waiver, which works as primary coverage when you decline the rental company's insurance, is another underappreciated benefit that can save $30 or more per rental day. At $95 a year with no foreign transaction fees, the Chase Sapphire Preferred remains one of the most complete packages available to travelers who aren't ready for a $550 annual commitment.
