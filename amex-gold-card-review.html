Who Is This Card Best For?

  • 🍽️

    Dedicated Diners & Foodies

    If you spend $500+ per month at restaurants, the 4x dining rate alone generates enough points to justify the annual fee. Add in the $120 dining credit and $84 Dunkin' credit and the card pays for most of its own cost before you even board a plane.

  • 🛒

    Grocery-Heavy Households

    Earning 4x at U.S. supermarkets on up to $25,000/year is one of the strongest grocery multipliers available. A household spending $600/month at the supermarket earns 28,800 points per year — roughly $576 in travel value — from that category alone.

  • ✈️

    Frequent Travelers Who Transfer Points

    The real magic of this card lives in Amex's transfer partner roster. Flyers who know how to move points to partners like Air Canada Aeroplan or ANA can squeeze 3–5 cents per point from transfers, making the 100K welcome bonus worth $3,000–$5,000 in premium cabin flights.

Who Should Consider Another Card?

  • ⚠️

    Those Who Won't Use the Credits

    The $325 fee only pencils out if you actively use the monthly credits. If Grubhub, Uber Eats, and Dunkin' aren't part of your routine, you're paying full price — and at that point a $95 Chase Sapphire Preferred likely delivers more net value.

  • ⚠️

    Cashback Purists

    Membership Rewards points require some effort to maximize — researching transfer partners, booking award space, understanding transfer ratios. If you'd rather earn a flat 2% cashback without any homework, a simple cashback card will feel far less complicated and still deliver solid returns.

Our Verdict

★★★★½ 4.5 out of 5

The Amex Gold has quietly become one of the best cards for everyday spending in the premium tier. Its 4x dining and 4x U.S. supermarket rates are genuinely category-leading, and the card's true effective annual fee — after you factor in the $120 dining credit, $120 Uber Cash, and $84 Dunkin' credit — drops to around -$99 for cardholders who use all three benefits consistently. That's a card that actually pays you to carry it.

Where the Gold separates itself from competitors is the quality of Amex's transfer ecosystem. Unlike cashback cards where value is fixed, Membership Rewards points are flexible currency. Transferring to Air Canada Aeroplan for partner awards, or to Singapore KrisFlyer for business class on United flights, can unlock redemption rates that dwarf the typical 1–1.5 cent baseline. For travelers willing to invest a few hours learning partner sweet spots, the welcome bonus alone can fund a transcontinental business class ticket.

The tradeoffs are real but manageable. The $325 annual fee requires discipline to offset, and the Amex network still faces occasional acceptance gaps at small domestic merchants. The card also lacks the airport lounge access you'd find on the Amex Platinum or Chase Sapphire Reserve, making it a better choice as a daily driver than a primary travel card. Pair it with a no-fee travel card for lounge access and you've built a formidable two-card setup.

Bottom Line

The Amex Gold is the best dining and grocery rewards card in its class — and for cardholders who use its credits, it effectively pays for itself while building a points balance worth thousands in travel.
