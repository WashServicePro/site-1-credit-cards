Card Benefits & Perks

Business-grade protections including one of the best cell phone coverage benefits available on any card.

Cell Phone Protection — up to $1,000 per claim, $100 deductible, max 3 claims per 12 months. Covers all phones on your monthly bill paid with the card. The highest cell phone protection limit available on any business credit card.
Trip Cancellation & Interruption Insurance — Up to $5,000 per person, $10,000 per trip for pre-paid non-refundable travel expenses. Covers illness, severe weather, and other covered events.
Primary Rental Car Insurance — Collision damage waiver applies as primary coverage (not secondary) when the rental is for business purposes. Saves the ~$15–$30/day rental counter CDW upsell on every business trip.
No Foreign Transaction Fee. Earn 3x and 1x points on international purchases without a 3% penalty. Essential for businesses with overseas suppliers, clients, or employees who travel internationally.
Employee Cards at No Additional Cost — Add unlimited employee cards for free. Employee spending earns the same rewards rates, all rewards pool together, and you can set individual spending limits per card.
Purchase Protection — Eligible purchases covered against damage or theft for 120 days (up to $10,000 per claim, $50,000 per year). Extended Warranty — adds 1 additional year to eligible U.S. warranties of 3 years or less.

Who Is This Card Best For?

Three business profiles where the Ink Business Preferred's category structure delivers exceptional ROI.

📣

Businesses That Spend on Digital Advertising

Earning 3x on search engine (Google, Bing) and social media (Meta, TikTok, LinkedIn) advertising is a genuinely rare benefit — most cards treat ad spend as miscellaneous at 1x or 1.5x. A business spending $4,000/month on digital ads earns 12,000 points/month, or 144,000 points/year at 3x — worth $1,800 in travel via the portal or potentially more through transfer partners. The $95 annual fee is paid back in the first week of ad spend.

✈️

Business Owners Who Travel Regularly for Work

The combination of 3x on travel, no foreign transaction fees, primary rental car insurance, and trip cancellation coverage makes this card a complete business travel tool. Primary rental coverage alone is worth $30+/day per rental — for someone who rents cars twice per month on business trips, that's $720+/year in saved fees against a $95 annual fee. The no-foreign-fee benefit ensures international suppliers and travel don't incur hidden charges on top of the exchange rate.

📦

E-Commerce and Product Businesses With Shipping Costs

Shipping earns 3x — a category that very few cards touch, and one that's highly relevant for product-based businesses, Amazon sellers, DTC brands, and any company shipping physical goods to customers. A business spending $3,000/month on shipping earns 9,000 points/month at 3x. Combined with internet, phone, and any advertising on the same card, these categories can collectively reach the $150,000 annual cap and generate 450,000 3x points in a single year.

Who Should Consider Another Card?

Honest cases where a different business card better matches the spend profile.

⚠️

Service Businesses Whose Spending Falls Outside the 3x Categories

If your primary business expenses are payroll, office supplies, professional services, meals, or equipment purchases that don't fall into travel, shipping, ads, internet, cable, or phone — you'll earn 1x on most of your spending. At 1x with a $95 fee, the Ink Business Preferred underperforms the Ink Business Unlimited (1.5x on everything, no fee) for general-purpose business spending. Know your category breakdown before choosing between them.

⚠️

High Spenders Who Will Exceed the $150,000 Annual Cap

The 3x categories are capped at $150,000 per year combined — after which all spending drops to 1x. For very high-volume businesses that routinely exceed this threshold in travel or advertising alone, the diminishing return on a $95 card may warrant considering a premium business card like the Chase Sapphire Reserve for Business (no cap, 3x travel) or evaluating whether an Amex Business Platinum's per-category earning fits the spend profile better.

Our Verdict

★★★★½
4.5 out of 5 stars

The Ink Business Preferred® is the best $95 business card available today, and it's not particularly close. The 100,000-point welcome bonus alone — worth $1,250 in travel via Chase's portal, or potentially $2,000+ through transfer partners — represents one of the largest first-year returns in the business card category. The $8,000 spend requirement in 90 days is higher than consumer card bonuses, but very achievable for a small business that routes normal operating expenses through the card. At a $95 annual fee against a $1,250+ welcome bonus, the card pays for itself many times over in year one before you've earned a single ongoing reward.

The ongoing earning structure is intelligently designed for the specific spending patterns of small businesses. Travel, shipping, internet, phone, and digital advertising collectively cover a high percentage of what most small business owners actually spend money on. The cell phone protection benefit — $1,000 per claim for all phones on your plan — is the best coverage available on any business credit card and directly relevant to any business owner who regularly upgrades or manages multiple employee phones. Primary rental car insurance saves real money every time a business trip involves a rental, with no action required beyond using the card.

The card's main limitation is the $150,000 annual cap on 3x earning, which constrains value for very high-volume businesses, and the lack of an intro APR, which makes it unsuitable for businesses that carry a balance. For businesses that pay their card in full each month and route their primary operating expenses through the categories covered, the Ink Business Preferred generates a level of return that premium consumer travel cards at three times the annual fee often can't match. It remains the foundational recommendation for small business owners entering the Chase ecosystem.

Bottom Line

The Ink Business Preferred® is the definitive small business card for Chase ecosystem builders — a 100,000-point welcome bonus, 3x on the categories that matter most to small businesses, and best-in-class cell phone protection, all for $95/year.
